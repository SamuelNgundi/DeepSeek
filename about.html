About Us

Empowering communities through sustainable banana farming, innovation, and inclusive value chains in Githunguri, Kenya.

Background and Mission

BanSoko is a grassroots initiative committed to transforming banana farming in Githunguri Constituency, Kiambu County, into a structured, modern, and profitable value chain. Although bananas have long been grown in the area, they were historically treated as a secondary crop, often sold raw at low prices with minimal support, infrastructure, or value addition. Farmers faced high post-harvest losses, unorganized production, poor market access, and a lack of recognition for bananas as a viable economic driver.

BanSoko was created to address these challenges. Bananas were chosen because they grow year-round, thrive in small spaces, and provide both food security and steady income. Our mission is to develop a sustainable banana ecosystem that increases farmer incomes, reduces waste, and creates dignified work opportunities for women, youth, and persons with disabilities.

We follow a full value chain approach, supporting every stage from land preparation and certified seedlings to harvesting, post-harvest ripening, fibre processing, product development, and waste reuse. Farmers are trained in intercropping, regenerative fertilization, pest control, smart irrigation, and climate-resilient farming. We promote high-yield, disease-resistant varieties such as FHIA 17 and Giant Cavendish to improve performance and market competitiveness.

BanSoko also seeks to reduce dependency on brokers by introducing solar-powered ripening centres. These units improve banana quality, reduce time to market, and allow farmers to earn better prices while stabilizing the local banana economy.

Through this integrated model, every part of the banana plant is put to use. Stems that were once discarded are now converted into fibre and used to make sanitary towels, textiles, and hair wigs. Organic waste is turned into compost and bio-fertilizer, creating a zero-waste, circular farming system that strengthens local food systems and environmental sustainability.

Our Partners

Our Partners

BanSoko works with a wide range of institutional and community partners who share in our mission to build a better banana economy:

  • Local farmer groups and cooperatives organize production, marketing, and farmer mobilization
  • Agricultural Sector Development Support Programme II (ASDSP II) provides technical assistance, training systems, and program alignment
  • The County Government of Kiambu supports us with agricultural extension services and local infrastructure
  • Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology (JKUAT) contributes research and innovation in banana fibre and agronomy
  • The Kenya Agricultural and Livestock Research Organisation (KALRO) advises on sustainable pest and disease control methods
  • Youth and women‚Äôs groups lead the fibre extraction, tailoring, and sanitary product production processes
  • Public participation forums, school outreach, and farmer days continue to grow awareness and community ownership

We are also exploring partnerships with international buyers, exporters, and technology providers to expand our impact beyond the county.

How We Work

How We Work

BanSoko‚Äôs work spans the full banana value chain. We support farmers from the moment they prepare land to the final processing and sale of products. This includes:

  • Training in sustainable agronomy, including intercropping, organic pest control, and smart irrigation
  • Use of certified seedlings, particularly FHIA 17 and Giant Cavendish
  • Post-harvest handling improvements using ripening chambers to stabilize prices and improve banana quality
  • Fibre extraction using specialized machinery to process banana stems into raw fibre
  • Tailoring and manufacturing of value-added products such as sanitary towels, textiles, and hairpieces
  • Composting and organic fertilizer production from banana waste

BanSoko promotes a circular model that minimizes waste and increases profitability across every stage. It creates opportunities for income generation, skills development, and long-term market participation.
