About Us

Background & Mission

BanSoko is a transformative agricultural initiative rooted in Githunguri Constituency, Kenya. Our mission is to empower banana farmers and rural communities through innovation, education, and value addition, while advancing the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Background: BanSoko was established to address the challenges faced by banana farmers, including low productivity, post-harvest losses, and limited market access. By leveraging partnerships and modern agricultural practices, BanSoko aims to create sustainable livelihoods and food security for all.

Mission: To uplift banana farmers and their families by providing access to quality inputs, training, and markets, while promoting environmentally friendly and inclusive development.

Our Partners

BanSoko collaborates with a diverse network of partners, including:

  • Farmer cooperatives and community groups
  • ASDSP II (Agricultural Sector Development Support Programme Phase II)
  • Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs)
  • Research institutes such as JKUAT (Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology) and KALRO (Kenya Agricultural & Livestock Research Organization)
These partnerships enable BanSoko to deliver training, research, and market access to farmers across the region.
