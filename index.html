Our Story

BanSoko began with a simple but powerful vision, to unlock the hidden potential of banana farming in Githunguri, Kiambu County. While the region is well known for dairy, tea, and coffee, bananas have long been grown here without structured support, market access, or value addition. Most farmers sold their bananas raw, earning little despite the crop‚Äôs high potential. This inspired the creation of BanSoko, an initiative born out of the community and championed by Hon. Gathoni Wamuchomba, Member of Parliament for Githunguri. Through BanSoko, farmers now receive training, access to certified planting materials, and support through every stage of production, from land preparation and smart irrigation to pest control and post-harvest handling.

But BanSoko is more than just farming. We‚Äôve introduced ripening centres to improve quality and market value, and pioneered banana fibre extraction to create eco-friendly products like textiles, sanitary towels, and wigs. Our inclusive model prioritizes women, youth, and persons with disabilities, and is fully aligned with key UN Sustainable Development Goals such as No Poverty (SDG 1), Zero Hunger (SDG 2), Gender Equality (SDG 5), Decent Work (SDG 8), and Climate Action (SDG 13). Today, BanSoko is transforming lives, reducing waste, creating jobs, and showing Kenya and the world what a single crop can achieve when innovation meets community.

Our Impact

BanSoko is delivering measurable results through smart banana farming, value addition, and inclusive rural development.
Here are just a few highlights of the transformation underway in Githunguri and beyond:

  • 8.6% of Kenya‚Äôs bananas are grown in Githunguri ‚Äî making it a national hub for banana production and innovation.
  • In 2023, Kenya produced over 2.9 million metric tons of bananas, with BanSoko-supported farmers contributing significantly to national food security.
  • Over 70% of banana plant waste, which once went unused, is now being converted into eco-friendly fibre and organic fertilizer.
  • We are targeting the creation of 1,000+ jobs across the value chain ‚Äî from farming and harvesting to fibre extraction and textile production.
  • All farming practices promoted by BanSoko follow regenerative, organic, and climate-smart principles ‚Äî improving soil health and boosting yields sustainably.
